Good to know:

How long does embroidery take?

Most orders ship or are ready for pickup in 5–10 business days. Rush available — ask us.

What file do I need to send?

We accept JPG, JPEG, and SVG files. Higher resolution files produce sharper embroidery results — 300 DPI or higher is recommended for JPG and JPEG files. SVG vector files provide the cleanest results at any size. Not sure what you have? Send it anyway — we will let you know if it needs adjustment.

DTF Printing

Full-color, photographic-quality prints applied directly to your shirt. No minimums, no color limits, no compromises. DTF is perfect when your design is too detailed for embroidery — gradients, photos, fine lines, and multi-color artwork all come out crisp and vibrant.

What you get:

  • T-shirts, tanks, hoodies, and long sleeves
  • Full-color artwork from your file
  • Small batches and one-off orders welcome
  • Customer-supplied or shop-sourced blanks
  • Fast turnaround on simple orders

Starting at $10/piece print + application — blanks from $4 each

1 / 5

Good to know:

What's the difference between DTF and screen printing?

Screen printing is great for large runs of simple designs. DTF is better for detailed art, photos, and small quantities — no setup fees, no minimums.

Is DTF durable?

Yes — wash inside-out in cold water and your print will stay vibrant for years.
